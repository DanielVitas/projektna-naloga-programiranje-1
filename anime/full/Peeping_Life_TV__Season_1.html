Synopsis

This "slow-life anime" is part of the datsuryoku-kei (ennui-style) genre of comedy that depicts the minutiae of everyday life. The series observes the daily lives of Heroes from the worlds of Tezuka Production and Tatsunoko Production, "evolved" for their modern TV appearances, and combines motion-captured improvised acting with computer animation into a series of comedy shorts.

(Source: ANN)

This is a concept show that bears little resemblance to normal anime -- get voice actors in a room, let them ad-lib some silly situations, then put together humorously bad 3D models based on characters from classic anime stories to deliver the lines -- add it all together and you get a strangely enjoyable comedy that I am very glad I picked up. There's really no way to accurately describe why it is fun except to say that if you like improv comedy at all, or even if you don't but you might get a giggle out of improv delivered by Casshern and Luna arguing about when they will set their wedding date, then this show is for you.

Story - 0.7/10

What can I say? The whole concept is supposed to be pretty cool seeing well-known Japanese characters doing what seems like stand-up comedy... But, it was leagues away from poor. The jokes are desert dry and colder than liquid nitrogen and I would have to be extremely drunk to find any of the jokes funny or even worse,... amusing. It had potential to be great but in this case it is the representation of complete lack of talent, originality and above all,... good humor.

Characters - 0.4/10

Take all your favorite characters and remove from them any kind of emotion and resemblance to a human beings, thus leaving only chunks of robot metal. That is a brief summary of characters in the anime. At least, their dialogue was interesting for only 1 second, the rest was like speaking with someone who has the intelligence and brain of a wall brick.

Art - 0.6/10

It is self-evident! The studio that produced this "anime" conveyed clearly to the viewers one thing. It did not even care trying to do something at least decent. The 3D figures are beyond horrendous and as for the rest it was as the producers of the show were bored of their lives and they just doodled!

Sound - 4.0/10

OP - The OP song was probably the only redeeming factor this show had.It is simply catchy, awesome and BRADIO! They know how to do amazing songs! Take for example Flyers, the OP for Death Parade. The track is simply great.

ED - Unfortunately, the ED was PeepingLife-icated and was boring and awful. It was like a woman just woke up with a horrible hangover from drinking 2 gallons of beer last night and started singing with utter boredom of living.

OST - No comment!

Enjoyment - 0.5/10

I think I already answered why I scored so lowly my personal enjoyment in the Story and the rest.

OVERALL: 1.24/10 (Oh and yeah I am still alive after this experience)

Recommendation: FEF

I would rather punish myself rewatching 5 times the whole series of Sword Art Online Season 1 than watch another episode from this atrocity (Atrocity is a euphemism in this case, but whatever!).

Thank you for your time reading this extensive review, add me as a friend, check out my list and please be respectful towards the opinions of others and mine.

*A red flame suddenly turns Indigo and then... the review is over* (FINALLY!)

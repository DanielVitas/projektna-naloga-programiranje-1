Released along side the Super S movie, Ami-Chan&rsquo;s First Love is a fun little romp through the Sailor Moon story. Clocking in at about fifteen minutes, including the new ending theme, which is rather catchy and fun to listen to, it is pretty much a little deviation from the main story. This turns out to help and harm this anime at the same time.

Whereas the TV series focuses mainly around Usagi, it is Ami that is at the center of attention this time around. She even starts off the movie by telling about what she likes and dislikes, as well as her hobbies and home life. As everything progresses, more of Ami&rsquo;s personality is shown; everything from her serious scholarly attitude to her comical and light-hearted side is shown in this short amount of time. It&rsquo;s nice to see that at least one of the other senshi has a deeper personality than what is shown otherwise. That&rsquo;s not to say that the others are left in the complete background. Everyone does their part to make this anime a nice little treat. The way the others try to help Ami is just as any friend would try to do, and it never fails to put a smile on my face and make me chuckle when I watch them. Though the plot may seem to be a little bit on the simpler side of the spectrum, it doesn&rsquo;t need to be complex at all. There&rsquo;s something in this anime for just about anyone: A nice story about friends, a little love thrown in, and plenty of light-hearted comedy.

At the same time, however, there just isn&rsquo;t enough time to make much anything else happen with the given amount of time. In essence, this is a mini episode of Sailor Moon, complete with transformation and attack, though none of this comes from Usagi at all. With that in mind, fans of the show and/or Sailor Mercury will get the most enjoyment out of this one, though just about anyone should be able to get some kind of enjoyment out of this anime. On the technical side, Ami-Chan is no slouch at all. The art and animation receives a nice boost, as colors are much brighter here than in the TV series, and though the music is the same from the TV series, it does its job just fine.

If you&rsquo;re a fan of the series or just in need of a fifteen minute deviation from life, give Ami-Chan&rsquo;s First Love a go. Who knows, maybe you&rsquo;ll remember how you reacted to your first love too.

This is an easy one to miss, since it was never officially translated to English and sort of sits outside of both the series and the movies, but it's absolutely worth hunting down. Ami's First Love is an animated short based on one of Naoko Takeuchi's "Exam Battle" short stories from the manga. It was aired along with the SuperS movie, and never made the jump across the ocean (Hopefully, Viz will get us an official translation this time around). This hidden gem of the series is short but sweet, with a lot of love put into it.

Ami's First Love is basically a slightly shorter Sailor Moon episode with a little more budget, so the animation is smooth and consistent. We get to see a pretty transformation and attack for Ami that are exclusive to the short. An original soundtrack was also written specifically for this special. The story itself is mostly comedic, but also a cute and personal exploration of Ami's character.

If you're a big fan of the Sailor Moon manga and want to see a really good manga-to-screen transition, you'll probably love this!

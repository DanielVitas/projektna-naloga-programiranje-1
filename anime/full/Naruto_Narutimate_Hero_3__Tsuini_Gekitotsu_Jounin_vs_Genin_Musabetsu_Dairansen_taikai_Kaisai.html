Synopsis

A contest is made by the Fifth Hokage called Jonin vs Genin. The point is to collect crystals for points, with the higher-ranked Chunin and Jonin holding crystals worth more points. The Genin have blue crystals, while the Chunin and Jonin have red crystals.

The video shows various fights between the Genin and Jonin, which each instance ending in the Jonin unknowingly losing their crystal (or discarding it).

(Source: Wikipedia)

In this special Naruto OVA, sparks fly. Literally.

In the special, Tsunade decides to hold this competition where Jonin must fight genin. Which seems kinda unfair, but whatever.

I was very annoyed at how it was handled. This OVA hinted at many things: That we would see some sweet Gaara action, that we would see Neji VS Asuma, that we would see Naruto and Sasuke team up against Kakashi. But this OVA didn't deliver. Every time something exciting happened, the scene would switch. WTH? And the endong ends in a total cliffhanger! Man!

This OVA isn't that good, so only fans of Naruto should even consider watching.

It was all an unsensible mess until Naruto broke the fourth wall and made an announcement for some upcoming game (at the time). It's a lengthy commercial your favorite show, yay! No, not really. Konoha is holding a global event for every Ninja to participate in, every individual is given a crystal neckless, and tasked with keeping it intact and take over the others' in hope of staying in the competition that's rewarding as a prize to make any wish they won't come true. Naruto and Sasuke, of course, start getting competitive and all, and set Kakashi as their target, hence the “Jonin” in Naruto Narutimate Hero 3: Tsuini Gekitotsu! Jounin vs. Genin!! Musabetsu Dairansen taikai Kaisai!!.
I haven't seen Naruto in a long time, but, from this, it's safe to assume that its way over the usual budget. The animation was fluid, colorful pallet and amazing particle effects.
I was one to experience part one Naruto in DUB, I had no prior knowledge on what the SUB sounded like, however, I was welcomed with generous open arms, the voice acting was just as high in quality if not better.
I had quite a happy time seeing all of those young characters before the demi-Gods era of late Naruto Shippuden and Boruto but still cringed very hard at every desperate attempt of throwing dull jokes, never change Naruto, never change.
